            Seasonal Information

            • Rivers & Waterfalls: Vernal Fall, Nevada Fall, Bridalveil Fall, and Yosemite Falls are still flowing well. Use extreme caution anywhere near flowing water; stay back from riverbanks and don't enter the water above waterfalls or rapids.
            • Tree work in Yosemite Valley: The Scenic Vista and Biomass Removal projects are underway in Yosemite Valley.
            • Bears: Bears remain active throughout the park, especially during the day around picnic areas, campgrounds, and river beaches. Be sure to store your food properly and to remain at least 50 yards from bears in forest and meadows (but yell at/scare away a bear that approaches you or is in a developed area).

            Trails and Wilderness Conditions

            Trails in Yosemite may be closed when there are hidden hazards or when they are impassable due to rock fall, snow, ice, or fire. Open trails aren't necessarily free of hazards--by entering the wilderness, you assume responsibility for your safety and must use good judgment. Trails not listed below are open, but may be snowy and very difficult to find from about November through May/June (and may have other hazards).

            Be extremely careful anywhere near rivers and creeks. Rocks adjacent to rivers and creeks are surprisingly slick and current remains strong. Most people who die in rivers and creeks didn't intend to be in the water. Think about where you will end up when you slip or fall (e.g., into a calm pool or into boulders, debris, rapids, or over a waterfall).

            Yosemite Valley Area

            • The Mist Trail from the John Muir Trail junction just uphill from the Vernal Fall footbridge to the top of Vernal Fall will be closed Mondays through Thursdays from 7 am to 3:30 pm from July 10 through September 1. From September 2 through November 2 (except holidays), the closure will be Mondays through Fridays (same hours; dates subject to change). The trail will be open Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays and will usually be open from 3:30 pm to 7 am (there may be some nights when the trail must remain closed). Vernal Fall, Nevada Fall, and beyond are reachable during the closure via the John Muir Trail and Clark Point cutoff trail. This closure is due to trail work. To hike on this trail during a day it is closed, you must be within the closure area by 7 am (you don't need to be all the way through the closure by 7 am).
            • The trail to Bridalveil Fall is closed due to the Bridalveil Fall Rehabilitation Project until fall 2023.
            • The Half Dome cables are up for the season. A permit is required 24 hours per day to hike to the top of Half Dome when the cables are up.

            Crane Flat to Tuolumne Meadows Area

            • Merced Grove of Giant Sequoias is closed through October for biomass removal.
            • Except for trails, the area north and west of North Dome is closed due to a fire. All trails in this area are open, but visitors must remain on trail through the burned area.
            • Elizabeth Lake Trailhead (in Tuolumne Meadows Campground) is closed. Start from the visitor center or Dog Lake parking areas instead (once Tioga Road reopens in summer).

            Climbing Closures

            Climbing closures are in effect on several routes each year from March 1 through July 15 to protect peregrine falcons. Additionally, a free wilderness climbing permit is required for all overnight big wall climbs.
